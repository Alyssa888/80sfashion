1980's Fashion Shoes

Converse Fast Break

These high-top sneakers were originally basketball shoes. However, just like a lot of other fashion items from the 80s, these too became essential everyday items. They were available in a lot of bright colors. Nevertheless, the red and the blue ones were the most popular. They were an important part of streetwear in the 80s. They were worn with rolled-up baggy jeans, tracksuits, and no-show socks.


Reeboks

Reeboks were available in many different colors including the white ones which were very popular. Not only did they serve as a comfortable sport shoe, but they also served as a fashionable sneaker that you could wear with almost anything you had. Thanks to their simple yet elegant look, people with all kinds of styles got themselves a pair. Take aerobics, casual everyday wear, and other sports for example, you would see almost everyone wear a pair of Reeboks. They wore them with bomber jackets, fanny packs, legwarmers and more.


Nike cortez

The Nike Cortez sneakers were available in a variety of colors and designs, allowing people to express their personal style. The classic on the other hand was the white Cortez with a red Swoosh. The sneakers were worn with high socks, often pulled up just above the ankle, and rolled-up jeans. The shoes originated form fitness fashion, but later were a must-have in any closet. People would style them casually with leggings, often brightly colored or patterned, crop tops, denim jeans or shorts, or with coordinating tracksuit bottoms and a sporty top.


Keds

These sneakers were a casual trend among both men and women. The canvas shoes with a rubber sole were seen as a symbol of comfort. They were worn with everyday attire such as denim jeans or shorts, graphic t-shirts. They could also be worn more preppy and sportier with tennis skirts or pleated skirts and paired with polo shirts or button-down blouses. The sneakers even became a huge hit for people who loved aerobics. You would see people with colorful leggings covered in bold patterns, and high-cut leotards. While white Keds were a classic, bold colors and even patterns began making their way to the big audience.
