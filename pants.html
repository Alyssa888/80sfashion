1980's Fashion Pants

Acid jeans

Characterized by their distinctive washed-out appearance, acid jeans featured irregular and bleached patterns, giving them a worn, almost tie-dye effect. One of the most iconic combinations was pairings them with oversized band T-shirts or graphic tees. For a more edgy look, acid-washed jeans were often styled with leather jackets. Acid washed jeans became the symbol of rebellion and self-expression. They were available in a range of colors. From classic blue to more distinct shades like pastel pinks and blues.


Mom jeans

Mom jeans are high-waisted jeans with tapered legs. In the 80s hey were usually worn with oversized sweaters and sweatshirts. For a more ‘cool and urban vibe’, mom jeans were often styled with bomber jackets or oversized denim shirts. Wide belts with statement buckles were also an important accessory styled on top of the mom jeans. Besides that, scrunchy socks worn with sneakers like Nike, Adidas, low-cut boots, or loafers were a popular choice for shoes.


Dungaree

Denim dungarees, or overalls, could be found in the wardrobes of both men and women. They were loved for their comfort, offering a laid-back yet stylish option. Many people in the 80s wore their denim dungarees with colorful bandanas tied around their heads or with baseball caps with logos or quirky slogans. Embracing the denim-on-denim trend, many fashion enthusiasts dared to wear denim dungarees with a matching denim jacket. This bold styling choice showcased the rebellious fashion trends that came to live in the 80s, breaking the traditional fashion rules.


Paperbag jeans

Paperbag jeans are known for their high-waisted design and a gathered or folded waistline. They earned their name from the resemblance of the gathered waistband to the top of a paper bag, creating a playful and ‘puffy’ silhouette. A popular way of styling them was pairing them with oversized, off-the-shoulder sweatshirts or slouchy sweaters, creating a relaxed yet stylish look. For a more polished and chic appearance, women often combined the jeans with fitted blouses or button-down shirts.
